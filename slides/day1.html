                       The study site is located within the Ethiopian highlands, in the Awash River Basin, 33 km West of Addis Ababa, plot size 9x6m. A field experiment was conducted for 3 consecutive years (2009\2002011) at the experimental farm of the Holeta Agricultural Research Centre (HARC) located at 09004\200N and 38029\200E at an elevation of 2400m above sea level. The study area receives high annual rainfall with a long-term annual average (1969 to 2014) of 1050mm per year. The rainfall has bimodal distribution that occurs in Kiremit (main rain) and Belg (minor rain) seasons. In general,>75% of the annual rainfall occurs in the main rain season (June to September) particularly in July and August. The experiment was conducted in clay texture soil with 58% clay content. The bulk density of the soil was 1.12 g cm\2103. The water content of the first 30 cm depth of the soil at field capacity (0.33 bar) and permanent wilting point (15 bar) were 32% and 24%, respectively. The plot size used for this experiment was a runoff plot of 9m length and 6m width.  The study area is crop-livestock farming system, where both livestock and crops are important agricultural practices. Cattle are important in the agricultural production system as they provide draught and threshing power, and manure to improve soil fertility and provide cow-dung for fuel. Crop residues are used as fodder and a source of domestic fuel. Wheat (Triticum astivum L.), teff (Eragrostis tef Zucc.), faba bean (Vicia fava L.) and field pea (Pisum sativum L.) are common crops grown in the study area.   The seeding rate of wheat (variety Kubsa) was 175 kg ha\2101 for broadcasting. The recommended fertilizer rates applied for all treatments were 69 P2O5 and 60 N per hectare. In zero and minimum tillage treatments, glyphosate herbicide was applied to control weeds. However, hand weeding was used to control weeds in conventional tillage treatments. Good coding habits

  • Put all library() calls at the top of the script.

  • Organize your files into data, notebook/markdown, output.

  • Comment your code.

  • Object names:
    Dont start with numbers
    Shortest understandable

  • Each %>% followed by a new line.

  • Avoid long lines (new line after comma).
